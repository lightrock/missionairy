Proposed Title:
MissionAiry: An AI Mind-Agnostic Mission-Closure Adaptor for Controlled Emergence in Heterogeneous AI Collectives

Abstract:
MediaNow, Inc. proposes MissionAiry, an AI mind-agnostic local adaptor architecture for TA1/TA2 DICE decentralized coordination and local inference control across heterogeneous AI agents operating under contested, degraded, and adversarial mission conditions. The core innovation is a finite mission-closure boundary that turns any reasoning engine and platform layer into an inspectable mission participant. Before an agent role, peer message, plan update, or inference output can affect collective behavior, the adaptor requires a closure receipt against authority, evidence, capability, energy, communications, time, consequence, role, and quality of service. This gives TA2 a measurable local-control object and gives TA1 an actionable invariant: agent reasoning may remain diverse and cognitively agile, but mission-relevant commitments are bounded, expiring, auditable, and revocable.

MissionAiry implements this boundary through two complementary overlays. PFEM, the Polycentric Federated Evidence Mesh, preserves evidence provenance, confidence, contradiction state, freshness, and audit receipts across local agents and rollup views. PFCOM, the Polycentric Federated Command Mesh, is the command-side complement to PFEM, preserving tasking, authority, role commitments, execution gates, fallback conditions, and accountability. Together, these overlays provide a common mission grammar while allowing different reasoning engines, autonomy stacks, sensors, platforms, and human authority nodes to participate through the same adaptor interface.

The proposed TA1 contribution is a peer-to-peer coordination method in which agents form local mission teams through bounded role offers, capability commitments, evidence receipts, and quality-of-service declarations. Agents may bid for roles, reject roles, delegate subtasks, or trigger replanning only when local closure conditions are satisfied. The TA1 planner consumes TA2-exposed validity intervals, role-coherence bounds, and expiration/degrade conditions to set planning horizons and replanning schedules. This supports distributed mission decomposition, resilient task reassignment after agent loss, and containment of compromised, stale, or contradictory peers without full global replanning.

The proposed TA2 contribution is a local inference-control method based on ZFA-style finite closure. ZFA, Zero Free Action, enforces that no agent action is treated as free: every proposed action must close against capability, authority, evidence, energy, communication state, time, consequence, and command scope. The formal object is a mission commitment record that binds a proposed output to its evidence, authority, role, validity interval, degrade rule, and revocation condition. This allows local inference control to operate across large models, small models, rule systems, planners, hybrid autonomy stacks, and degraded controllers without requiring access to identical internal representations.

MissionAiry will evaluate the adaptor using an internal evaluation harness designed for compatibility with DICE TA3 integration. The harness separates degradation of the AI reasoning layer from degradation of the hardware/platform layer, enabling controlled tests of role drift, hallucinated or unsupported claims, compromised agents, stale context, degraded sensors, intermittent communications, failing platforms, and partial command updates. The simulation will include heterogeneous mission agents representing mobile platforms, sensors, communications relays, logistics resources, human authority nodes, local policy agents, evidence agents, and adversary/failure agents. Initial scenarios will emphasize contested mission support and forward critical-infrastructure status because these long-horizon domains require decentralized evidence fusion, local policy, intermittent communications, and independent rollup reasoning while remaining generalizable to broader DoW-relevant mission settings. Evaluation will compare MissionAiry against centralized orchestration baselines across scalability, time-to-recovery, mission alignment, role coherence, adversarial robustness, and useful cognitive diversity.
