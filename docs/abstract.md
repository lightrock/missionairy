## MissionAiry Abstract

Defense is the long-term mission under emergence. Offensive autonomy can be episodic, but defensive autonomy must persist: watching, adapting, coordinating, absorbing shocks, restoring function, preserving authority, and maintaining trust while threats, technologies, infrastructure, and human conditions change around it.

MissionAiry is a common mission operating environment for rapidly integrating, validating, updating, and redeploying emerging AI and autonomy capabilities while keeping mission surfaces human-comprehensible for authorities and operators. It treats defense not as a fixed shield, but as a living mission posture across humans, infrastructure, sensors, communications, emergency networks, and heterogeneous autonomous agents.

Today, autonomous offense is creating a severe cost imbalance. Cheap drones and rapidly evolving agent capabilities can force expensive, brittle, or delayed defensive responses from missiles, fixed counter-drone appliances, scarce operators, and overloaded command structures. Offense alone does not level this field; critical infrastructure, communications, logistics, and civilian continuity still have to be defended.

MissionAiry addresses this gap by coordinating defensive sensors, observers, interceptors, autonomous patrols, emergency communications, evidence flows, authorities, rules of engagement, and updateable mission plans inside a common operating environment. The goal is to make defensive autonomy cheaper, faster, more distributed, more accountable, and more resilient under rapid capability emergence.

MissionAiry is especially relevant to critical infrastructure, emergency communications, and HAM/RACES-style resilience networks, where fragile communications, local improvisation, distributed expertise, and public accountability matter as much as the autonomous systems themselves. Its central claim is that the future defensive advantage will not come from any single drone, sensor, missile, jammer, or laser station, but from the disciplined ability to understand, integrate, validate, update, and redeploy defensive mission capabilities at operational speed.
